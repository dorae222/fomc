For release at 2:00 p.m. EDT March 22, 2023 Recent indicators point to modest growth in spending and production. (0.531)

Job gains have picked up in recent months and are running at a robust pace; the unemployment rate has remained low. (0.575)

Inflation remains elevated. (0.556)

The U.S. banking system is sound and resilient. (0.764)

Recent developments are likely to result in tighter credit conditions for households and businesses and to weigh on economic activity, hiring, and inflation. (0.724)

The extent of these effects is uncertain. (0.599)

The Committee remains highly attentive to inflation risks. (0.487)

The Committee seeks to achieve maximum employment and inflation at the rate of 2 percent over the longer run. (0.612)

In support of these goals, the Committee decided to raise the target range for the federal funds rate to 4-3/4 to 5 percent. (0.505)

The Committee will closely monitor incoming information and assess the implications for monetary policy. (0.816)

The Committee anticipates that some additional policy firming may be appropriate in order to attain a stance of monetary policy that is sufficiently restrictive to return inflation to 2 percent over time. (0.688)

In determining the extent of future increases in the target range, the Committee will take into account the cumulative tightening of monetary policy, the lags with which monetary policy affects economic activity and inflation, and economic and financial developments. (0.783)

In addition, the Committee will continue reducing its holdings of Treasury securities and agency debt and agency mortgage-backed securities, as described in its previously announced plans. (0.572)

The Committee is strongly committed to returning inflation to its 2 percent objective. (0.552)
